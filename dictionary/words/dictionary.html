dictionary

/ˈdɪkʃən(ə)ri/

early 16th century: from medieval Latin dictionarium (manuale) or dictionarius (liber) ‘manual or book of words’, from Latin dictio (see [diction].

Definitions:

  • Noun
    • a book or electronic resource that lists the words of a language (typically in alphabetical order) and gives their meaning, or gives the equivalent words in a different language, often also providing information about [pronunciation], origin, and usage.
    • a reference book on a particular subject, the items of which are typically arranged in alphabetical order.
    • Computing a set of words or other text strings made for use in applications such as [spellcheckers].
