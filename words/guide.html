guide

/ɡʌɪd/

late Middle English: from Old French guide (noun), guider (verb), of Germanic origin; related to wit2.

Definitions

  • verb
    • show or indicate the way to (someone).
    • direct the motion or positioning of (something).
  • Noun
    • One who shows the way by leading, directing, or advising.
    • One who serves as a model for others, as in a course of conduct.
