method

/ˈmɛθəd/

late Middle English (in the sense ‘prescribed medical treatment for a disease’): via Latin from Greek methodos ‘pursuit of knowledge’, from meta- (expressing development) + hodos ‘way’.

Definitions:

  • a particular procedure for accomplishing or approaching something, especially a systematic or established one.
  • the quality of being well organized and systematic in thought or action
